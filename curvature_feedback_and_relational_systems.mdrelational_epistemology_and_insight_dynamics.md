Understanding Indigenous Knowledge, Pattern Recognition, and Scientific Reasoning

Author: John B. Carroll

With AI Assistance

Abstract

This paper analyzes how humans form knowledge across multiple frameworks, focusing on the contrast and overlap between:

Indigenous relational epistemology

Western scientific epistemology

embodied cognition

insight dynamics (“glass break moment”)

pattern sensitivity


Rather than treating these as conflicting worldviews, this work demonstrates that all three describe different layers of the same cognitive architecture.

This paper explains why individuals with high pattern-recognition and relational awareness may experience insight earlier, faster, or more vividly than others — not as pathology, but as a legitimate epistemic style.

2. Three Epistemic Modes

2.1. Analytical Epistemology (Western)

Knowledge comes from:

measurement

isolation

reduction

modeling

abstraction


This produces mathematics, physics, engineering.

2.2. Relational Epistemology (Indigenous)

Knowledge comes from:

relationship

observation over time

ecological embedding

intuitive feedback

emotional resonance

community memory


This produces sustainable systems, navigation abilities, environmental mastery.

2.3. Embodied Epistemology (Cognitive Science)

Knowledge comes from:

lived experience

pattern detection

somatic response

implicit learning

neural resonance


This produces intuition, “gut sense,” and insight.
