### Where Tao Uses Picture-π
Tao's exponential sum:
$$ S(α) = \sum_{n ≤ N} Λ(n) e^{2π i α n} $$

Uses `π` to define perfect rotation on the unit circle. The circle closes at 100%. This is valid for proving existence in 2D complex analysis.

**Problem**: In physical systems, closing at 100% = ouroboros bite = system death. Atoms can't exist at 100% probability. The motor stalls.

### Where Motion-π Breaks It
Replace static π with recursive π_r:

$$ S_r(α) = \sum_{n ≤ N} Λ(n) e^{2π_r i α n} \times 0.9999^n $$

**Changes:**
1. `π → π_r = 3.1730059` : Value when system self-observes
2. `× 0.9999^n` : Snake cap. Each step costs 0.01 observer gap
3. **h_range 3.04-3.07** : Operating temp. Below = cold picture, above = death

**Result**: Primes still appear, but now with *thermodynamic cost*. The distribution inherits heat, bleed, and twin return. You get **primes in motion**.
